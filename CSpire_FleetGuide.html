🚨 Accident & Damage Reporting
⚠ Most Important Rule
Make NO statements about fault or liability. Be courteous but say nothing that could imply responsibility.
At the Scene
1
Ensure your safety and any passengers. Dial 911 immediately.
2
Seek medical help for anyone injured, or if law enforcement recommends it.
3
Take photos of all vehicles, the entire area, traffic signs, signals, skid marks, and traffic direction.
4
Collect: other driver's name, license number, insurance carrier, policy number, phone number.
5
Show the responding officer the vehicle insurance card. Get the collision report number, officer name and badge number.
Report Same Day — Required
!
Email FleetAccidentReporting@cspire.com — include date, your name, vehicle make/model/tag, photos, police report, injuries, and whether vehicle is drivable.
!

🔧 Maintenance & Inspections
Driver Responsibilities
  • Conduct a visual pre-trip inspection before driving each day.
  • Ensure all inspections, registrations, and insurance certifications are current and valid.
  • Report needed repairs promptly — do not operate any vehicle you believe to be unsafe until corrected.
  • Follow all preventive maintenance schedules per manufacturer specifications.
  • Ensure annual vehicle tag renewals are completed and affixed.
Critical Components — Always Maintain
Recalls, timely oil changes, brakes, tires, suspension, steering, lights, mirrors, windows, and windshield wipers must always be controlled, maintained, and promptly repaired.
Negligence Warning
Failure to complete required vehicle maintenance may result in financial responsibility for repair costs and disciplinary action up to and including termination.
✅ Daily Pre-Trip Inspection
Required Every Day Before Driving
Conduct a visual pre-trip inspection before beginning each driving day. Report any unsatisfactory results to your supervisor before operating.
DOT Inspection Checklist (CMV Drivers)
  • Service brakes, including trailer brake connections
  • Parking (hand) brake
  • Steering mechanism
  • Lighting devices and reflectors
  • Tires — condition and inflation
  • Horn
  • Windshield wipers
  • Rear-vision mirrors
  • Coupling devices
  • Wheels and rims
  • Emergency equipment
Post-Trip — CMV Drivers
Complete and sign a Driver Vehicle Inspection Report (DVIR) at the end of each day for each vehicle operated. List any defects found. Original DVIRs are collected weekly by managers and retained for at least 3 months.
🪪 Authorized Drivers
Who Is Authorized
  • C Spire employees assigned as the primary driver of a fleet vehicle.
  • Other C Spire employees with intermittent or occasional need to drive on behalf of the Company.
Who Is NOT Allowed to Drive
  • Relatives or domestic partners of the employee
  • Non-authorized business associates
  • Contractors
  • Friends or acquaintances
  • C Spire employees not expressly authorized

Exception: A non-authorized driver may only drive in a medical or safety emergency if legally licensed in their state.
Policy Reminder
Permitting unauthorized use may result in disciplinary action up to termination and financial responsibility for all associated repair or replacement costs.
📊 Driving Record Requirements
Annual MVR Review
C Spire reviews Motor Vehicle Records at least annually. Failing any criteria below means you cannot drive a company vehicle or on behalf of the company.
Disqualifying — 3-Year Window
  • More than 2 moving violations within 3 years
  • More than 1 at-fault collision within 3 years
  • 2 moving violations + 1 at-fault collision within 3 years
Major Violations — 5-Year Window
Any single occurrence of: DWI/DUI, refusing substance test, open container, reckless driving, hit and run, fleeing police, racing, driving with a suspended/revoked license, vehicular assault or manslaughter, or using a vehicle in connection with a felony.
⚠️ License Suspension
Report Immediately
Any driving infraction that results in loss of driving privilege must be reported to your Manager and Human Resources immediately — even if a temporary driving permit has been issued.
What Happens Next
A valid driver's license is an essential function of your role as an authorized driver. Employees with a suspended license may face disciplinary action up to and including termination, depending on the circumstances.

The manager of the previously authorized driver is responsible for securing the vehicle on C Spire property for the duration of the suspension.
🚦 Traffic Violations
Who Is Responsible for Citations?
C Spire is not responsible for traffic violations or parking tickets. Citations are the employee's personal responsibility — even when received while conducting company business.
Reporting Requirement
Drivers must immediately report any ticket, arrest, collision, or damage to their manager — including incidents that occur during personal time.
⚖️ Driver Negligence
Financial & Disciplinary Risk
Driver negligence may result in up to 100% financial responsibility for repair costs and disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.
Examples of Negligence
  • Failure to promptly report an accident or significant damage
  • Failure to initiate repair in a timely and authorized manner
  • Failure to complete required vehicle maintenance on schedule
  • Failure to keep the vehicle clean inside and out
  • Failure to properly secure the vehicle and its contents at all times
  • Failure to comply with vehicle recall requirements from the manufacturer
🔒 Theft Prevention
Required Theft Prevention Measures
  • Never leave your vehicle running and unattended at any time.
  • Never leave keys in the car or ignition — even inside a locked garage.
  • Always roll up windows and lock the vehicle when unattended, including at home.
  • On job sites: never leave doors unlocked with keys in the vehicle when unattended or unoccupied.
  • When possible, park in view of high-traffic, well-lit areas.
  • In a fee garage, take the pay-ticket with you.
  • Remove valuables or hide them out of sight before exiting the vehicle.
  • If doors will not lock, the driver and management team are responsible for having repairs made promptly.
🔑 Key Control
Key Issuance Rules
Keys are issued only to C Spire employees by Corporate Purchasing. Department managers may authorize keys for pool vehicles.

No key may be duplicated without Corporate Purchasing approval. Unauthorized duplication adversely affects security and will result in disciplinary action.
Lost or Stolen Keys
Report to Security and Corporate Purchasing immediately by the quickest means available. If stolen, file a formal police report and follow up with the Security Manager. The employee and their manager are responsible for obtaining a replacement key when authorized.
🚫 Prohibited Activities
Strictly Prohibited In or On Company Vehicles
  • Transportation of hazardous materials (unless DOT-approved for a C Spire business purpose)
  • Mounting unauthorized permanent devices — cell phone holders, roof carriers, etc.
  • Unauthorized customized license plates, stickers, decorations, temporary paint, or banners
  • Defacing or altering the vehicle in any manner
  • Smoking of any kind — tobacco, e-cigarettes, marijuana/cannabis (whether legalized or not), even with windows down
  • Transportation of hitchhikers
  • After-market remote car starters
  • Pets are not permitted in fleet vehicles
  • Unauthorized roof racks or carriers for luggage, skis, bicycles, or kayaks
  • Radar detectors
🚘 Rentals & Leased Vehicles
Same Rules Apply
All C Spire Fleet Management policies apply equally to rental and leased vehicles in addition to company-owned fleet vehicles. The same safe driving, reporting, and conduct standards are in effect.
Insurance Card Required
Drivers must have a current copy of C Spire's "Leased/Rented Vehicles" insurance identification card before driving. It must be available to present to law enforcement when requested.
🚫 Alcohol & Drug Policy
Zero Tolerance
Under no circumstances may a driver operate a C Spire vehicle while impaired by alcohol, illegal drugs, or legal drugs that impair driving ability. Violation results in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
Reporting Requirement
Employees cited or convicted of driving under the influence must report the citation or conviction to their supervisor within 24 hours — regardless of whether the vehicle was personal, rental, or company-owned.
Post-Accident Testing
An alcohol and/or drug test may be administered after a collision at the discretion of Human Resources.
📵 Distracted Driving
What Counts as Distracted Driving
Talking on a phone without handsfree, texting, emailing, programming a GPS, using headphones for audio devices, or any activity that takes your eyes or attention off the road — including eating and drinking.
What You Must Do Instead
  • Pull over safely to the shoulder before engaging in any distracting activity
  • Use a hands-free kit or Bluetooth device for all phone calls
  • Follow all applicable state and local laws
  • Never use any electronic device with headphones while driving — illegal in most states and prevents hearing warning signs, signals, or sirens
Keep a Safe Following Distance
Always maintain a safe following distance between you and the car in front of you. Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
🏗️ DOT Commercial Driver Program
Does This Apply to You?
DOT regulations apply if your vehicle transports more than 15 passengers, has a GVWR of 26,001+ lbs, has a GVWR of 10,001+ lbs in interstate commerce, or carries hazardous materials requiring placarding.
CDL Driver Requirements
  • Pre-employment DOT drug screen before operating in a safety-sensitive function
  • Random drug and alcohol testing throughout the year
  • Valid Medical Examiner's Certification — must be maintained while in the role
  • Self-enroll in the National Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse (NDAC)
Hours of Service Summary
  • 10 consecutive hours off duty required before driving
  • Maximum of 11 hours driving during a 14-hour on-duty window
  • 30-minute rest break required within any 8-hour driving period
  • Maximum 60 hours on duty in any 7 consecutive days
  • Maximum 70 hours on duty in any 8 consecutive days
Annual DOT Inspection
Every commercial vehicle must have a periodic inspection at least once every 12 months per 49 CFR 396.17. The original or a copy must be retained in the vehicle for no less than 14 months from the report date.

📄 Fleet Management Policy
Official Policy Document
This is the complete, unabridged C Spire Fleet Management Policy. Revised April 30, 2026. Policy Owner: Corporate Purchasing.
Overview

C Spire may provide a Company vehicle to eligible employees who have an ongoing business need for ground transportation to perform their job. It is the responsibility of each authorized driver to ensure he/she is both familiar with and following the specifications of this policy. Authorized drivers must operate Company vehicles entrusted to them in a responsible, safe, legal, courteous, and non-abusive manner, in accordance with this policy, and with state and federal laws.

Employees are responsible for maintaining logs related to vehicle utilization (including daily personal and business miles driven) and for the regular maintenance of the provided vehicle in accordance with preventive maintenance best practices and as outlined in this policy. In addition, to maintain a professional image and protect the resale value of the vehicle, employees are responsible for keeping the vehicle clean inside and out. All accidents, regardless of fault, must be reported to @fleetaccidentreporting on the date of accident occurrence.

Failure to comply with the policies contained herein by any authorized driver may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. Managers are responsible for issuing disciplinary action for policy violations, following consultation with Human Resources.
Eligibility for a Company Vehicle

Only employees authorized by the Company will be granted the use of a Company vehicle. Authorization for the issuance and continued use of a Company vehicle must come from the department EVP or CXX. As a rule, Company vehicles should only be authorized for individuals who routinely drive over 1,200 miles per month for business purposes. Average business miles driven will be monitored by Corporate Purchasing, and management will be requested to regularly reaffirm the continued need for vehicles that are being driven fewer than 1,200 miles per month.

Authorized Drivers

The procedures within this policy apply to all employees operating a Company fleet vehicle, their personal vehicle, or a rental vehicle on behalf of the Company.

Only authorized drivers may operate Company vehicles that they are licensed to operate. Authorized drivers are defined as:

  • C Spire employees who have been assigned as the primary driver of a fleet vehicle.
  • Other C Spire employees who are not assigned a vehicle, but who may have intermittent or occasional need to drive on behalf of the Company.

Company vehicles may not be driven by: (a) relatives or domestic partners of the employee, (b) non-authorized business associates, (c) contractors, (d) friends or acquaintances, or (e) C Spire employees who have not been expressly authorized to drive a Company vehicle. A non-authorized driver may drive a Company vehicle only in the event of a medical and/or safety emergency and only if the individual is legally licensed in his/her state of residence.

Permitting unauthorized use of a Company vehicle may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment for the authorized driver to whom the vehicle was issued. Additionally, authorized drivers may be responsible for all repair or replacement costs associated with non-authorized use.
Basic Vehicle Operation Guidelines

Employees are expected to treat Company vehicles with an appropriate level of respect and care, and to consistently adhere to all traffic laws and C Spire standards for safety.

  • All drivers and passengers are expected to follow local, state, and federal guidelines for safe operation of a vehicle.
  • No employee shall drive while under the influence of alcohol or drugs, including illegal substances or medication that may impede the employee's ability to operate a vehicle safely.
  • The proper use of seat belts is mandatory.
  • Employees should focus on driving defensively, maintaining a focused awareness of their surroundings and of other drivers in proximity.
  • Never move through traffic aggressively.
  • Comply with speed limits and traffic signs. Follow all traffic signals.
  • Use extreme caution when driving through construction and school zones. Stop as required by law when school buses are picking up or dropping off passengers.
  • Always lock the vehicle and apply the parking brake when getting out, even if it remains in sight.
  • During long trips, take breaks every four hours. Never drive more than 10 hours during a 24-hour period.
  • When possible, avoid driving past midnight.
  • Employees shall not drive when drowsy.
  • When possible, drivers should avoid operating the vehicle in dangerous conditions, including flooded roads, icy conditions, heavy fog, and inclement weather.
  • Properly dispose of trash promptly, which not only maintains brand standards but also removes items that may hamper safe operation.
  • Never drive distracted which includes the use of a cellular phone without handsfree operation, eating, drinking or any other activity that takes your attention away from driving safely.
  • Radar detectors are prohibited in C Spire vehicles or C Spire rental vehicles.
  • Smoking, vaping, or the use of any tobacco products is not permitted when driving on behalf of the Company, or at any time in Company fleet vehicles.
  • Tracking devices have been installed on each Company vehicle and should remain properly plugged in at all times. Any issues with devices becoming loose or non-usable should be promptly reported via email to the Fleet Program Manager.
Collision and Damage Reporting and Instructions

If you are involved in an automobile collision while in a Company vehicle (or a rental vehicle), be courteous, but MAKE NO STATEMENTS regarding responsibility, fault or liability. Additionally:

  • Take all reasonable steps necessary to ensure your immediate safety and that of any passengers in your vehicle and others at the scene.
  • Dial 911 to report a vehicle collision.
  • Seek medical treatment for anyone injured if you believe it is needed or if recommended by law enforcement.
  • Contact a member of the C Spire Fleet Management team or the Safety team as soon as practical (FleetAccidentReporting@cspire.com). All accidents or vehicle damage must be reported on the date that the accident or damage occurs.
  • TAKE PHOTOS OF VEHICLES AND THE ENTIRE AREA: RECORD TRAFFIC DIRECTION, TRAFFIC FLOW, SIGNALS, SIGNS, SKID MARKS, ETC.
  • If law enforcement requests details, ask to speak to the officer in private and provide a factual description of what you observed. DO NOT speculate. DO NOT assume under any circumstances that you were legally at fault or make statements to that effect.
  • DO NOT discuss the details of the collision with anyone other than law enforcement at the scene or elsewhere without first obtaining permission from C Spire Fleet Management, the Safety team, or C Spire's Legal team. This includes the other driver, investigators, attorneys, claims adjusters, or media representatives.
  • DO NOT describe the details of the collision in any written correspondence, including email, social media, or memorandum, unless asked to do so by C Spire Fleet Management, the Safety team, C Spire's Legal team, or a Company attorney.
  • Provide or show the responding officer(s) the vehicle insurance card.

Collect the following information related to other driver(s): Driver name, Driver's license number(s), Insurance carrier name(s), Insurance carrier phone number(s), Insurance policy number(s).

Obtain a copy of the collision report completed by the police officer. If unavailable at the time, collect: Collision report number, Officer's name and badge number, Police station phone number.

Report all vehicle collisions or damage to FleetAccidentReporting@cspire.com and your supervisor as soon as it is safe to do so, but no later than end of day. Report to Wheels (formerly Donlen) same day at 800-323-1483.

An alcohol and/or drug test may be administered after a collision at the discretion of Human Resources.

Driver/Manager Expectations
  • Managers must ensure that drivers have received, understand, and have acknowledged receipt of the Fleet Management policy, including Appendix A – Distracted Driving, Appendix B – Fleet Commitment Statement, and the Use of Company Vehicles policy before driving a Company vehicle.
  • Managers must provide notice of all vehicle assignments by emailing @CSpireFleet before a vehicle is swapped, exchanged, assigned, or reassigned.
  • Drivers must always operate vehicles in a safe manner following the Basic Vehicle Operation Guidelines.
  • Drivers and managers must ensure their assigned vehicles are always clean (both on the interior and exterior) to ensure compliance with the Company's brand image and as a safety best practice.
  • Drivers and managers must ensure annual vehicle tag renewals are completed and affixed, as well as ensure compliance with state required inspection sticker regulations, where applicable.
  • Drivers must maintain a valid driver's license appropriate for the type of vehicle assigned.
  • Drivers must maintain an eligible driving record.
  • Drivers must participate and complete any required safe driver training.
  • Drivers must immediately report a ticket, arrest, collision, or damage to their manager in accordance with the Collision and Damage Reporting and Instructions section. This includes tickets or collisions which occur during the employee's personal time.
  • Drivers who have a corrective lenses restriction on their driver's license are responsible for adhering to such restrictions.
Driver Negligence

Driver negligence may result in the authorized driver assuming financial responsibility for up to 100% of repair costs deemed as having resulted from driver negligence, and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Examples of driver negligence include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Failure to promptly report an accident or significant damage to your manager and/or proper authorities.
  • Failure to initiate repair of the vehicle in a timely and authorized manner.
  • Failure to complete required vehicle maintenance according to the maintenance schedule.
  • Failure to keep the vehicle clean, inside, and out.
  • Failure to properly secure the Company vehicle and its contents at all times including, but not limited to, laptops, cell phones, demo equipment and other Company property.
  • Failure to comply with vehicle recall requirements provided by the manufacturer.
Conditional Employee Eligibility to Drive

C Spire will review each driver's Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) at least annually to determine whether that driver meets minimum requirements for safe driving or is disqualified to drive a Company vehicle. An employee will not be eligible AND will not be allowed to drive a Company vehicle or on behalf of the Company if she or he:

  • Has more than two (2) moving violations within a three-year period.
  • Has had more than one (1) at-fault collision within a three (3) year period.
  • Has two (2) moving violations and one (1) at-fault collision within a three (3) year period.
  • Has had one major moving violation [DWI/DUI/OUI, refusing substance test, driving with an open container (alcohol), reckless driving, hit and run, fleeing or evading police or roadblock, resisting arrest, racing/speed contest, driving with license suspended or revoked, vehicular assault, homicide or manslaughter or using vehicle in connection with a felony] within a five (5) year period.
  • Has a driver's license in a suspended or ineligible status.
Fuel Card

A fuel card is assigned to each vehicle with a fixed dollar limit and must be secured by the driver.

  • The purchase of gasoline higher than 87 octane is prohibited unless required by the vehicle manufacturer.
  • Team members to whom Company vehicles are assigned will be issued a fleet fuel card, which should be used for all Company vehicle fuel purchases. Personal credit cards or other forms of payment for fuel should only be used in emergency circumstances and with approval from their manager or from the Fleet Program Manager.
  • The fuel card may not be used to purchase fuel for any other vehicle, to purchase any unauthorized vehicle accessories (i.e., battery jumper kit), or to purchase miscellaneous personal items such as food, drinks, and newspapers. Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment for the authorized driver.

If a fuel card is lost, stolen or damaged, the authorized driver must contact @CSpireFleet immediately to have the card blocked and to have a replacement card issued.

Inspections and Maintenance

To maintain the safety and integrity of the vehicle, C Spire will provide the necessary resources to ensure all vehicles are operating properly. All routine motor vehicle maintenance will be done according to the manufacturer's specifications. Critical components that must always be controlled, maintained, and promptly repaired include, but are not limited to, recalls, timely oil changes, brakes, tires, suspension, steering, lights, mirrors, windows, and windshield wipers.

  • It is the driver's responsibility to ensure routine maintenance practices are followed and to report other needed repairs timely. Drivers shall not operate any vehicle they believe to be unsafe until the unsafe condition is properly addressed and corrected as needed.
  • Drivers are required to conduct visual pre-trip vehicle inspections before beginning driving each day. Any unsatisfactory result requires a notification to the employee's immediate supervisor for further guidance on how to proceed.
Key Control

Issuance of a Company vehicle key will be completed by Corporate Purchasing. Department managers may authorize the issuance of keys in the case of a Company pool vehicle.

Keys will be issued only to C Spire employees. No key may be duplicated except upon approval of Corporate Purchasing. The unauthorized duplication of keys adversely affects the security of persons and property, and violations of this rule will result in appropriate disciplinary action being taken.

Lost or stolen keys must be reported to Security and Corporate Purchasing by the quickest means available. Individuals who report keys as stolen are expected to file a formal police report and to follow up with the Security Manager.

License Suspension

Any driving infraction that results in loss of driving privilege for the authorized driver must be reported to the employee's Manager and Human Resources immediately regardless of whether a temporary driving permit has been issued.

Driving and possessing a valid driver's license are considered essential functions of an authorized driver's job. Employees whose driver's license is suspended may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, based on the facts and circumstance of the suspension. In the event of a license suspension, the manager of the previously authorized driver is responsible for securing the vehicle on C Spire property.

Restrictions

The following activities are strictly prohibited in or on a Company vehicle regardless of whether the vehicle is being driven for business or personal use:

  • Transportation of hazardous materials (unless for a C Spire business purpose and using DOT approved containers).
  • Mounting of unauthorized permanent devices in or on the vehicle (e.g., cell phone holders, roof carriers, unless for a C Spire business purpose).
  • Use of unauthorized customized license plates, stickers/magnetic signs, decorations, temporary paint, banners etc.
  • Fleet vehicles may not be defaced or altered in any manner.
  • Smoking is prohibited (this includes all forms of tobacco products, electronic/vapor cigarettes, use of marijuana/cannabis – whether legalized or not, and smoking with the windows down).
  • Transportation of hitchhikers.
  • Utilization of an after-market remote car starter.
  • Pets are not permitted in C Spire fleet vehicles.
  • Use of unauthorized equipment that is permanently mounted on the vehicle such as roof racks and carriers for luggage, skis, bicycles, and kayaks is strictly prohibited.
Refueling Guidelines

Vehicles should be refueled when the meter reads ¼ full. For your safety when operating a vehicle, follow these guidelines:

  • Turn off the vehicle's engine while refueling.
  • Never smoke, light matches, or use lighters while refueling.
  • Do not get into the vehicle during refueling, as this presents a flash fire hazard.
  • Do not overfill or top off the vehicle's fuel tank. The fuel dispenser shuts off automatically when the tank is full.
  • Never force the hold-open latch on the gasoline pump with any means other than the latch provided.
  • Do not leave vehicles unattended while fueling.
Rentals or Leased Vehicles

Operational guidance within C Spire's Fleet Management policies also apply to the operation of rental or leased vehicles. Drivers must have a current copy of C Spire's "Leased/Rented Vehicles" insurance identification card before driving, and available to present when requested by law enforcement.

Traffic Violations

C Spire is not responsible for traffic violations or parking tickets issued for violation of city ordinances or state/federal laws. Citations to an employee are the employee's responsibility, even if the citation is received while conducting business for C Spire.

Use of Alcohol or Drugs

Under no circumstances is a driver permitted to operate a C Spire Company vehicle while impaired by alcohol, illegal drugs, or legal drugs which may impair one's ability to drive. Any violation of this policy will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Employees who are cited or convicted of operating a vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs must report the citation or conviction to their supervisor within 24 hours; this requirement applies whether the employee was charged while driving a personal, rental or Company vehicle.

Termination

Upon termination, C Spire requires that the terminating employee return all vehicle keys to their manager for safekeeping until the vehicle is reassigned. An employee's termination will not be administratively complete until the manager has confirmed the return of assigned keys.

Theft Prevention Measures

C Spire requires all Company Drivers to follow these theft prevention measures:

  • Never leave your vehicle running and unattended at any time.
  • Never leave your keys in the car or ignition, even inside a locked garage.
  • Always roll up your windows and lock the vehicle if the vehicle will be unattended, including while at home.
  • On the job site, it is never acceptable to leave doors unlocked and keys in the vehicle at any time while the vehicle is unattended or unoccupied.
  • When possible, park in view of high-traffic, well-lit areas.
  • If you park in a fee garage, take the pay-ticket with you.
  • Remove valuables or hide them out of sight before exiting the vehicle.
  • If doors will not lock it is the responsibility of the driver and his management team to have repairs made in a prompt manner.
Appendix A — Distracted Driving Policy

The purpose of the Distracted Driving Policy is to ensure that all employees know the rules that apply to the use of a Company vehicle and/or driving on behalf of the Company.

Employees driving any Company Vehicle or an employee driving on behalf of the Company shall not drive distracted. Distractions include talking on a cellular phone without handsfree operation, texting, emailing, programming a GPS, audio device use requiring headphones including radios, MP3 players and similar items, or any other activity (including eating) that may take your eyes off the road and attention away from safe driving. Always KEEP A SAFE FOLLOWING DISTANCE BETWEEN YOU AND THE CAR IN FRONT OF YOU.

If you must engage in one of these activities, you are required to use one of the risk management steps below:

  • Pull over on the shoulder only when you have a safe place to do so.
  • Use a hands-free kit, Bluetooth device.
  • Follow all state and local laws.

Employees may not under any circumstances use any electronic device with headphones. Not only is it illegal to do so in most states, but headphones also impede a driver's ability to properly hear warning signs, signals, or sirens.

Failure to adhere to these policies may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
Appendix B — Fleet Commitment Statement

The purpose of the Fleet Commitment Statement is to ensure that all employees understand the expectations that apply to the use of a Company vehicle and/or driving on behalf of the Company.

All authorized drivers will adhere to the driver practices and written policies as established by the Company. Drivers also agree to follow all federal, state, and local laws applicable to operating a motor vehicle.

Motor vehicle operators will ensure professional appearance and upkeep of any vehicle representing the Company. Vehicles are expected to be kept clean, damage free and maintained within the preventative maintenance guidelines within this policy.

Motor vehicle incidents and moving violations must be reported to Corporate Purchasing and Safety on the date of their occurrence, or within 8 hours of the incident.

  • I understand that any motor vehicle used for the representation of the Company may NOT be operated by an unauthorized operator.
  • I understand the expectation to not drive distracted, including the use of a cellular phone in handsfree mode only.
  • I acknowledge I have received, read, and signed the Distracted Driving Policy.
Failure to adhere to these policies may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
